MENTISGATE WEBSITE - SIMPLE SETUP GUIDE
======================================

HOW TO OPEN THE WEBSITE ON YOUR COMPUTER
----------------------------------------
1. Do not open index.html while it is still inside the ZIP window.
2. Right-click MentisGate-Website-Ready-to-Upload.zip.
3. Click "Extract All".
4. Choose a folder and click "Extract".
5. Open the extracted MentisGate-Website-Ready-to-Upload folder.
6. Double-click index.html. It will open as the designed website in Chrome.

If the page appears as plain black text on a white background, it is still
being opened from inside the ZIP. Extract the complete folder first.

HOW TO PUBLISH IT ON MENTISGATE.ONLINE
--------------------------------------
1. Sign in to the control panel supplied by your website hosting company.
2. Open File Manager.
3. Open the public_html folder for mentisgate.online.
4. Upload MentisGate-Website-Ready-to-Upload.zip.
5. Extract the ZIP inside public_html.
6. Move everything from the extracted MentisGate-Website-Ready-to-Upload folder directly
   into public_html. index.html must be directly inside public_html.
7. Visit https://mentisgate.online and test the website.

HOW TO ENABLE THE ENQUIRY FORM EMAIL
------------------------------------
The website already contains send-query.php. You do not need to edit it.

1. Confirm these email accounts exist and can receive normal emails:
   partnerships@mentisgate.online
   customercare@mentisgate.online
2. Ask your hosting support team to do the following:

   "Please enable the PHP mail() function for mentisgate.online and confirm
   that emails sent by send-query.php can use
   customercare@mentisgate.online as the From address. Please also confirm
   that SPF and DKIM are enabled for mentisgate.online."

3. After they confirm, open the website and submit one Partnership enquiry.
   It should arrive at partnerships@mentisgate.online.
4. Submit one Customer Care enquiry. It should arrive at
   customercare@mentisgate.online.
5. Check Spam/Junk if the first test does not appear in the inbox.

If your hosting company says PHP mail() is not supported, ask them to enable
SMTP email sending. SMTP needs the outgoing mail server details supplied by
your hosting company. Their support team can configure it or provide those
details for a future update.
